Displaced Composition

I wrote a composition called "Displaced" to raise awareness of the refugee crises. But I didn't want to make it political. I wanted to make it relatable by positioning it as a humanitarian crises and not a political one.

I believe that music can reach into people's hearts, more so than words or images. So, I wrote a piece that was rooted in the sentiment of uncertainty and not quite belonging. We all can relate to that feeling. That's how I reached my performers and by extension the audience.

It was also a symbolic performance because I wrote the piece to include classic, choral and jazz themes and that brought in performers from a variety of groups/cliques in my school.

All of us coming together in that way to share in a common experience for a common cause was unifying and powerful.
